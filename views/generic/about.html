{% extends '../layout.html' %} {% set path = '/about' %} {% block content %} Verily is an experimental web application for crowdsourcing information verification for disaster response.

It was featured in Foreign Policy, New Scientist and MIT Technology Review, as well as in a TedX talk on Digital Humanitarians.

Verily was designed by Carlos Castillo (QCRI), Patrick Meier (QCRI), Victor Naroditskiy (Southampton) and Iyad Rahwan (Masdar).

Development of the platform is spearheaded by Victor Naroditskiy and Enrico Costanza at the University of Southampton, thanks to the enthusiasm, technical expertise and relentless work of Luis Arenal Mijares, Alex Greenland and Dimitrios Papamilios, who are students of the Web Technology MSc programme.

In July 2014 we are running the first public trial of Verily, thanks to the effort of Justine Mackinnon.

Verily is supported, in part, by the ORCHID project.

For more information about Verily please contact us at contact@veri.ly
