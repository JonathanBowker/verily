About

Verily is an experimental web application for crowdsourcing information verification for disaster response. It was featured in Foreign Policy, New Scientist and MIT Technology Review, as well as in a TedX talk on Digital Humanitarians.
Development of the platform is led by Victor Naroditskiy and Enrico Costanza at the University of Southampton, thanks to the enthusiasm, technical expertise and relentless work of Alex Greenland, Dimitrios Papamilios and Luis Arenal Mijares, who are students of the Web Technology MSc programme.
Verily was designed by Carlos Castillo (QCRI), Alex Greenland, Dimitrios Papamilios, Luis Arenal Mijares, Patrick Meier (QCRI), Victor Naroditskiy (Southampton) and Iyad Rahwan (Masdar).
In July 2014 we are running the first public trial of Verily, thanks to the effort of Justine Mackinnon.
Verily is supported, in part, by the ORCHID project.
For more information about Verily please contact us at contact@veri.ly
